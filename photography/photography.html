FILM PHOTOGRAPHY

Something about me and photography & film-making…

​

During my sophomore year, I participated in the Dare to be Digital game design competition. Our game immersed the player in a storyline that followed the journey with a kind old man.

I approached the shot language systematically, incorporating a multitude of professional features and aesthetics. With montages, jump cutting, and the critical influence background-music serves in a film, my desire to integrate these aspects in my future work was consolidated.

The narrative approach empowers films with palpable tension and can evoke the deeper emotions of audiences. These emotional elements are what I seek to convey in my following information design projects. My finished projects have given me an enlightenment that design needs to resonate with users, and that, by opening the emotional channel, design is able to yield more meaningful results.

I take short clips every now and then, and occasionally edit and combine them into videos to chronicle my life and record my evolving interests over time.

​

Honor: I participated in “The unspeakable openness of things - Olafur Eliasson” held at RED BRICK Art Museum on August 2018 with one partner, and our work(please scroll down to view) won the finalist.

ZHIXIU LIU

I’m Gloria, a first-year master student at Computer Science Department, Stanford University. ▌ Previously I studied at Tsinghua University and obtained my first master’s degree in Computer Science and Information Art and Design, during which I was broadly exposed to collaborative projects with the industry, involving user experience design, UI design and so on. ▌ My research projects range from business and serious games, to inclusive design with a focus on visually impaired people, then to the interaction between user and data. ▌ Though seemingly a mixed background, I was always trying to move towards the balance between users’ emotions and their effective productivity, as well as how design and technology can be better combined to this endeavor.
